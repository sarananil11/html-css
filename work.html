NISSAN SKYLINE GTR

The **Nissan Skyline** is one of Japan’s most iconic performance cars, renowned worldwide for its sleek design, advanced technology, and racing heritage. Originally launched in the late 1950s as a modest family car, the Skyline evolved over decades into a symbol of Japanese engineering excellence, especially with its legendary **GT-R** variants. Models like the **R32, R33, and R34 GT-R** earned the nickname “Godzilla” for their dominance on the track and in street racing culture. Equipped with the robust **RB26DETT** twin-turbo inline-six engine and the sophisticated ATTESA E-TS all-wheel-drive system, the Skyline GT-R became a dream car for enthusiasts and tuners alike. Immortalized in video games and movies like *The Fast and the Furious*, the Skyline remains a timeless icon that represents the pinnacle of Nissan’s performance legacy.

RPM

The stock RB26DETT redlines at around 8,000 RPM, which was very impressive for a turbocharged engine from the early 1990s. This high redline allows the Skyline to deliver power smoothly across a wide rev range, making it both responsive and thrilling to drive.
